Software Development Process

The software development process refers to the method via which an individual or a team design and builds a software, from its initial planning phase to its implementation or delivery to the client. The process either follows a straight-forward method of tackling problems, known as the waterfall model, or it follows the newer and more versatile method called agile software development. Whichever method is used, the core concepts and steps remain more or less the same. The steps are:

  • Requirements/ data gathering
  • Planning and design
  • Implement the code
  • Test the program
  • Maintain the software

All of these steps together are called the software development process.

Importance of Software Design

Software design is the process via which the client requirements and demands are turned into a plan to build a functioning program. In this stage of the software development process, programmers have to plan and figure out how they can respond to client requirements. This stage is necessary to make sure the resulting software is high-quality and user-friendly. The more thought-out and detailed the design stage is, the less errors the final product will have.

Personally Observed Software: Clip Studio Paint Mobile

The software that comes to my mind when I think of something “designed well” is Clip Studio Paint Mobile. It’s an illustration app designed for smartphones and tablet. The most important thing that stands out to me is how optimized the app is compared to other similar app on mobile, even on older hardware. The app rarely lags. It has both a simple mode for beginners and studio mode for advanced users. The simple mode is easy on the eyes while the studio mode has all the functions a professional may need. The app also lets you save, work and export your files in multiple different formats, which allow for a more versatile workflow. The app is a prime example of a software designed specifically to meet its userbase’s demands.

Requirement Gathering: Process and Importance

TRequirement or data gathering is the first and most important process in the software development process. This step gives a clear view of the demands of the clients and helps to establish a plan for the entirety of the development process. To start gathering the requirements, the developer or the company must ask the clients about their needs using clear and precise questions. The requirements gathered should clearly define the users, their needs and the actions taken by the application to satisfy those need. It should also be categorized based on their importance and priority and must be written in a simple but well-organized way. Without a clear understanding of this step, the developed software may turn out to be lacking or perhaps even entirely unfit to fulfil the demands of the client. As such, it’s important to be aware of the needs of the clients and gather requirements accordingly.

User Stories: Clip Studio Paint

The app I had mentioned previously was clip studio paint mobile. Five user stories related to the app are given below:

  • As an artist, I want a brush category, so that I can categorize and organize my brushes
  • As an artist, I want a liquify tool, so that I can shift and move around parts of the artwork without changing the layer contents.
  • As an animator, I want a timeline function, so that I can see the frames and timing of the animation clearly.
  • As an editor, I want a text wrap tool, so that I can fit the text to various objects and elements in the picture.
  • As an animator, I want an onion-skin function, so that I can observe the placements of my previous and next frames.
